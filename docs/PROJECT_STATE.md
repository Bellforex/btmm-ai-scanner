## 3. Rules Ready for Coding (precise, numeric, book-sourced)

- Order Block: 2-candle structure, displacement candle ≥2×/3× the smaller candle, located at origin of a move, zone = smaller candle's range.
- Fair Value Gap: 3-candle gap geometry (Buy: 3rd-candle-low > 1st-candle-high; Sell: 3rd-candle-high < 1st-candle-low).
- Engulfing Pattern: 2-candle structure, ≥2×/3× ratio, body-covers-body rule, explicit "middle of a move" location rule (vs. Order Block's "origin" rule), timeframe strength ranking.
- Buy-to-Sell/Sell-to-Buy Candle: ≥2×/3× ratio vs. average of preceding candles, explicit fail-then-reverse logic, full-candle-range zone.
- Base Rally/Base Drop: Base High/Base Low formula, departure candle ≥2×/3× the largest base candle, plus (provisional) 2–6 candle count, Base Height, midpoint drift, and overlap-ratio rules from Base Formation Standard V1 — Provisional.
- Morning Star/Evening Star: 3-candle structure, final candle ≥2–3× the middle doji.
- Previous/Current Period High-Low levels (12 variants): pure price-extreme detection over defined calendar windows, explicit timeframe strength ranking (month > week > day).
- Trendline: "at least two touches" confirmation rule (partially precise — steepness and swing quality remain open, see ambiguity register).
- BTMM: the 3-pillar model (Volume/Speed/Accuracy) and the core liquidity mechanic ("close buy = sell liquidity," "close sell = buy liquidity") are structurally precise even though their numeric thresholds are not yet set.

**Meaningful Swing High and Swing Low Detection Standard Version 1 — Provisional — Approved.** The author has resolved Ambiguity 10 ("meaningful swing"), explicitly as a **provisional** standard pending future calibration. Approved standard: a five-candle local pivot window (2 confirmed candles each side of the candidate/plateau); local Swing High/Low candidates qualify on **wick high/low** (never close/body/colour) after applying Pivot Tie Tolerance = `MAX(2×MinTick, 0.02×ATR14)`; adjacent candles within tolerance form one pivot plateau (Pivot Price = extreme across the plateau, Start/End Time preserved) rather than separate events. After the two right-side candles close, a candidate is `LOCAL_SWING_CANDIDATE` — not yet usable by Equal Highs/Lows, Trendlines, Support, Resistance, or other market-structure logic. Before meaningful confirmation, a more extreme same-direction candidate `SUPERSEDES` the prior one (never deleted from audit data). Once meaningfully confirmed, the pivot **never repaints** — price/times are locked and the swing is only available to downstream logic/backtests from `meaningful_confirmation_time` onward, preventing look-ahead bias. Confirmed swings must alternate direction (no two same-direction confirmed swings without an opposite one between them); HH/HL/LH/LL labels are **not** defined. Strength: `STANDARD_SWING` at ≥0.50×ATR reversal (the confirmation threshold itself); optional `STRONG_SWING` at ≥1.00×ATR "before the Pivot Price is materially breached" — **the exact numerical meaning of "materially breached" is explicitly left unresolved** (a sub-rule gap within this ambiguity, not tracked as a separate main ambiguity number). **Bullish and Bearish Trendline Detection and Validation Standard Version 1 — Provisional — Approved.** The author has resolved Ambiguity 11 ("trendline too steep"), explicitly as a **provisional** standard pending future calibration. Approved standard: eligible anchors are limited to `CONFIRMED_MEANINGFUL_SWING` events (per the Meaningful Swing Standard, Ambiguity 10) of the matching direction — two Swing Lows for a Bullish Trendline (Anchor 2 Price > Anchor 1 Price + Pivot Tie Tolerance), two Swing Highs for a Bearish Trendline (Anchor 2 Price < Anchor 1 Price − Pivot Tie Tolerance); prices within tolerance are `HORIZONTAL_CANDIDATE`, not a trendline. Anchor 2 must be ≥5 confirmed bars after Anchor 1. Raw Slope = `(Anchor 2 Price − Anchor 1 Price) ÷ (Anchor 2 Bar Index − Anchor 1 Bar Index)`; Line Price(t) = `Anchor 1 Price + Raw Slope × (t − Anchor 1 Bar Index)`. Normalized Slope = `ABS(Raw Slope) ÷ Anchor Reference ATR` (median ATR(14) across Anchor 1–Anchor 2): `HORIZONTAL_CANDIDATE` (<0.02), `VALID_SLOPE` (0.02–0.35), `TOO_STEEP` (>0.35, rejected as a candidate). Trendline Touch Tolerance = `MAX(2×MinTick, 0.10×ATR14)`; Trendline Pierce Tolerance = `MAX(2×MinTick, 0.20×ATR14)` — apply only to Trendlines, never replacing POI/Support/Resistance/Equal-Highs-Lows tolerances. Inter-anchor integrity rejects a pair if any confirmed candle between the anchors closes beyond the projected line by more than Pierce Tolerance (wick-only crossings within tolerance are fine). A fully-passing pair becomes `DRAFT_TRENDLINE`, available only from Anchor 2's `meaningful_confirmation_time`; a third qualifying same-direction confirmed meaningful swing (touch or controlled pierce) promotes it to `CONFIRMED_TRENDLINE`; a fourth promotes it to `STRONG_TRENDLINE`. `TRENDLINE_BREAK_CANDIDATE` is recorded on a Pierce-Tolerance-exceeding close beyond the line but is explicitly **not** final invalidation. Multiple trendline candidates may coexist per timeframe; none are silently deleted/replaced, and no "Best/Primary Trendline" or composite score is created. Non-repainting: anchors/slope never move or get refit; DRAFT/CONFIRMED/STRONG are each available to backtests only from their own confirmation timestamps. Full formulas in `knowledge/MEASUREMENT_STANDARDS.md`, "Bullish and Bearish Trendline Detection and Validation Standard."

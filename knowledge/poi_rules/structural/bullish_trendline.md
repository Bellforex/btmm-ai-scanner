# Bullish Trendline

## Alternative book name

NOT DEFINED IN BOOK.

## Family

Structural POI

## Direction

Bullish (ascending; acts as a buying POI).

## Source pages

"Trendlines" (paragraphs 576-589).

## Book definition

A diagonal form of support where price has reacted at least twice; a bullish trendline can act as a buying POI.

## Required market location

Diagonal, connecting swing lows within an uptrend.

## Formation conditions

At least two touches to establish the line.

## Confirmation conditions

The third or fourth touch can create a buy opportunity.

## Origin candle or level

The connected swing points (not a single candle).

## Upper boundary

Not numerically defined - the trendline itself is the level; no stated tolerance band for how close a touch must be to count.

## Lower boundary

Not numerically defined (same as Upper boundary).

## Wick treatment

Not defined in the book (unclear whether touches are measured by wick or by close).

## Body treatment

Not defined in the book.

## Candle-size requirement

Not applicable / not defined - trendlines are not candle-size-based POIs.

## Volume or momentum proxy

Not defined in the book.

## Trend requirement

By definition IS a trend-following tool; requires an underlying directional move to draw from.

## Market-structure requirement

Must connect to 'meaningful swing points where price has reacted clearly' - but swing-point detection itself is undefined (Ambiguity 10).

## Liquidity requirement

Liquidity is said to build around the trendline from other traders' orders/stops watching it, but no numeric rule is given.

## Timeframe requirement

Stronger on the 4-hour timeframe and above; timeframe choice should match trade type (scalp = lower TF, day/swing = stronger TF).

## Strength classification

Not numerically defined beyond "not too steep" (Ambiguity 11) and touch count.

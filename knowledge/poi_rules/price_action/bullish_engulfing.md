## Book definition

A two-candle reversal pattern where a larger bullish candle's body covers/engulfs the prior smaller bearish candle's body, showing sellers losing control to buyers.

## Required market location

Must appear WITHIN/in the middle of an existing price movement - the explicit rule distinguishing it from Order Block, which sits at the origin of a movement.

## Formation conditions

Previous candle relatively small and bearish; engulfing candle bullish and larger, covering the previous candle's body.

## Confirmation conditions

Engulfing candle must close bullish (close above open) with aggressive buying pressure; a stronger version also engulfs the previous candle's entire range including wicks.

## Origin candle or level

The two-candle pair.

## Upper boundary

Not explicitly given as a standalone zone formula distinct from the pattern's own candles (contrast with Order Block's explicit 'Zone = Smaller Candle Low to High'). Recorded as a gap.

## Lower boundary

Not explicitly given as a standalone zone formula (see Upper boundary note).

## Wick treatment

Body-engulfment is the baseline rule; a stronger version also engulfs the previous candle's wicks, but this is not required for the baseline valid pattern.

## Body treatment

Core rule: the engulfing candle's body must cover/engulf the previous candle's body.

## Candle-size requirement

Size Ratio (Total Range) >= 2.0 (standard) / >= 3.0 (strong) vs. the previous candle, per Measurement Standard V1. The previous (engulfed) candle's "smallness" is now also governed by Small Candle Standard V1 (Ambiguity 2, resolved): its Total Range <= 0.50x the engulfing candle's Total Range (standard) / <= 0.3333x (strong), with a separate secondary Recent Market Context classification that does not override this. Comparison target per the approved standard: the immediately preceding candle. See knowledge/MEASUREMENT_STANDARDS.md, "Small Candle and Recent Market Context Standard."

## Volume or momentum proxy

Same open question as Ambiguity 3.

## Trend requirement

Stronger when it appears after a pullback in an uptrend - contextual, not an absolute requirement for pattern validity.

## Market-structure requirement

Explicit warning: traders must not trade every bullish engulfing pattern without checking market structure, trend, liquidity, and location.

## Liquidity requirement

Stronger when it appears after a liquidity sweep or at a POI/support/demand zone - contextual, no formal numeric rule.

## Timeframe requirement

Explicit ranking: Daily > 4H > 1H > 15-minute.

## Strength classification

Standard (>=2x) / Strong (>=3x); Higher-timeframe vs. Lower-timeframe (lower TF 'may require confirmation from market structure, liquidity, trend direction, or a higher-timeframe POI').
